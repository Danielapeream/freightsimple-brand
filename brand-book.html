01 — Brand Foundation

Who we are

FreightSimple is the friendly, capable colleague who makes LTL freight shipping simple and enjoyable — not the corporate, heavy-handed solution that adds complexity.

Mission

"FreightSimple is your LTL Freight Department — an extension of your shipping team that handles everything so you can focus on your core business."

Positioning

We're exclusively LTL experts — not freight generalists. We understand LTL shipping better than anyone, offering instant quotes, guaranteed pricing, automatic bill auditing, and anomaly detection that no other broker has.

Our style in one sentence

"Modern, clean, professional, slightly playful — light and airy, not heavy or oppressive."

Professional and credible, but warm and approachable. Think friendly colleague, not enterprise software.

04 — Tone of Voice

How we sound

Professional but never stiff, friendly but never casual, honest but never blunt. We sound like a trusted, capable colleague — not a salesperson or an enterprise software company.

Clear & simple
We say what we mean
No jargon, no fine print. Use plain language and explain freight terms when needed. If something is complex, make it simple. Always lead with customer benefits, not features.
Friendly & warm
We're on your team
Use "we" and "you" to create connection. Think "your buddy in LTL freight." Warm, approachable, like a helpful colleague — never cold or corporate.
Confident
We know our stuff
We're exclusively LTL experts. Write with authority and confidence, but never arrogance. Back claims with specifics — "automatic bill auditing" not "we might save you money."
A little playful
Freight can be fun
Professional but with a touch of playfulness. "Stop babysitting shipments" is perfect — memorable, a little witty, completely on-message. Don't be afraid to have personality.
Words we love "Simple" — "just works" — "your buddy" — "on your team" — "LTL freight" (always specify LTL) — "we" and "you" — "in minutes, not weeks"
Words to avoid Jargon without explanation — "leveraging" — "solutions" — "synergies" — passive voice — vague claims without proof — "terms and conditions may apply"
Say this
"The price you see is the price you pay — no surprises after delivery."
"We catch problems before they blow up."
"Stop chasing carriers. We track every shipment so you don't have to."
"Your core competency is manufacturing — not LTL freight. We handle LTL so you can focus on what you do best."
"Real humans who pick up the phone. Not bots."
Never say this
"Leveraging best-in-class logistics solutions to optimize your supply chain."
"Rates subject to change based on carrier availability and accessorial charges."
"Our platform facilitates seamless freight procurement workflows."
"Please be advised that delivery windows are estimated and non-guaranteed."
"We handle FTL, Intermodal, Parcel, and LTL freight solutions."

11 — Photography & Imagery

How we look

Every image we use is a brand statement. FreightSimple's visual world is clean, human, and grounded in real work — never generic stock, never heavy industrial. Light, modern, and honest.

The FreightSimple visual feeling
☀️
Light & airy
Well-lit spaces, natural light, open environments. Never dark, moody, or dramatic. Images should feel easy and approachable — like our brand.
🤝
Human & real
Real people in real work environments — warehouses, loading docks, offices. Not overly staged or polished. Authenticity over perfection.
✂️
Clean & minimal
Uncluttered compositions. Clear subject. Generous negative space. If an image feels busy or overwhelming, it's not right for FreightSimple.
What to show — image subjects
Use these types of images
People working — warehouse staff, logistics managers at their desk, small business owners reviewing shipments. Real work, real people.
Clean warehouse interiors — organized shelving, pallets, loading docks. Well-lit, tidy, modern facilities.
Freight in motion — trucks on highways, cargo being loaded, packages on conveyor belts. Energy and movement.
Technology moments — someone on a laptop or phone using FreightSimple, screens showing clean UI, data visualizations.
Canadian context — recognizable Canadian settings, diverse teams, Canadian businesses and landscapes when relevant.
What to avoid
Never use these
Cliché stock photos — handshakes, people in suits pointing at whiteboards, generic "business success" imagery.
Dark, gritty, industrial imagery — rusty warehouses, dim lighting, heavy machinery. Too heavy for our brand.
Overly staged or fake-looking photos — everyone smiling perfectly at the camera, artificially diverse "diversity stock."
Competitor branding, logos, or trucks in frame — never show a specific carrier's branding prominently.
Low resolution, pixelated, or watermarked images — always use high-resolution licensed photography.

12 — Iconography

Icons & symbols

Icons are functional — they help users navigate, understand features, and scan information faster. FreightSimple icons follow a consistent style that matches our clean, modern, friendly brand.

Icon style principles
Line style
Outline icons only — never filled solid icons. 2px stroke weight. Matches our light, clean aesthetic.
Rounded ends
Always round line caps and joins. Never sharp, angular corners — matches our friendly, approachable personality.
Simple shapes
Minimal detail, clear at small sizes. If an icon needs to be complex to be understood, it's the wrong icon.
Consistent size
Standard sizes: 16px (inline), 20px (UI), 24px (cards), 32px (features), 48px (hero). Never mix sizes in the same context.

Icon do's
Always use outline/line icons — consistent with the Lucide Icons library
Use 2px stroke weight — matches our light, clean visual language
Use rounded line caps and corners on all icon strokes
Color icons in brand blue #4C62EA on light backgrounds
Use white icons on dark or blue backgrounds
Keep icons at standard sizes: 16 / 20 / 24 / 32 / 48px
Icon don'ts
Never mix icon styles — don't use filled icons alongside outline icons
Never use multiple different icon libraries in the same product or document
Never resize icons to non-standard sizes (e.g., 18px or 22px)
Never use overly complex or detailed icons that don't simplify at small sizes
Never use emoji as interface icons — only in informal content like Slack
Recommended library Lucide Icons (lucide.dev) — open source, MIT license, outline style, rounded corners, 2px stroke. Available as React components, SVG files, and a Figma library. This is the only approved icon library for FreightSimple interfaces and documents.
